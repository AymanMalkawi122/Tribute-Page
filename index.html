Alan Turing

The man who revolutionized computers as we know them today

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life

  • 1912 - Alan Mathison Turing was born on June 23, 1912 in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1926 - At the age of 13, Alan Turing first showed signs of intelligence and potential which was noticed at the Sherborne School.
  • 1931 - Alan Turing studied from 1931 to 1934 at King's College, in Cambridge. He was first in his class and was an honor student in the field of mathematics.
  • 1936 - Turing studied mathematics and cryptology while also building three of four stages of an electro-mechanical binary multiplier at Princeton University.
  • 1940 - Turing was responsible for creating the Bombe which was a cryptanalytic machine used to decrypt the Enigma-enciphered messages being sent from the enemies.
  • 1947 - Turing lived in Hampton, London where worked on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine).
  • 1948 - Turing created the LU decomposition method that was used and is still used today to solve matrix equations.
  • 1948 - Turing was appointed a position in the mathematics department at the University of Manchester where he worked on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers.
  • 1950 - The turing test was created in 1950. The turing test was created to compare the intelligence of a machine/computer to the intelligence of a human brain.
  • 1954 - dies at the age of 41
